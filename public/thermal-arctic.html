Thermal Imaging in Arctic Conditions: What to Look For

When temperatures plummet below -40°C and whiteout conditions erase all contrast, visible-light optics fail. Thermal becomes your only reliable sense.

1. Thermal Sensitivity (NETD) – The Most Critical Spec

NETD (Noise Equivalent Temperature Difference) measures how well a sensor detects tiny temperature differences.

  • Consumer-grade: 50–70 mK → marginal in fog/snow
  • Military/pro-grade: <40 mK → detects body heat through blowing snow at 400+ m
  • Best in class: <25 mK → sees heat signatures in near-zero contrast environments

Rule of thumb: For Arctic ops, never settle for >45 mK.

2. Resolution & Detection Range

In flat, featureless tundra, higher resolution = earlier positive ID.

  • 384×288: Marginal beyond 250 m
  • 640×512: Reliable human detection to 500–700 m
  • 1280×1024: Overkill for most, but ideal for scanning wide sectors

3. Cold-Weather Battery Survival

Lithium-ion capacity drops 50–80% below -30°C. Real solutions:

  • CR123A primary lithium cells (hold charge down to -40°C)
  • External heated battery pouches (body-heat activated)
  • Devices with hot-swappable 18650/21700 cells — rotate warm spares
