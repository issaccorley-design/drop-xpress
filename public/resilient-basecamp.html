Building a Resilient Base Camp in Remote Areas

A good base camp buys you time, security, and recovery. A bad one costs you everything.

1. Site Selection Checklist

  • Elevation advantage — High ground for observation, drainage, and defense
  • Natural cover — Tree line or rock outcrop for concealment
  • Water proximity — 100–300 m (close but not too close — reduces insect/mosquito exposure)
  • Avoid flash flood zones — No dry riverbeds or low ground in monsoon areas

2. Perimeter & Security Layers

  1. Outer layer — Trip wires + noise makers (cans, bells) at 50–100 m
  2. Middle layer — IR trip beams or pressure plates (if tech available)
  3. Inner layer — Sleeping area inside natural cover, 360° visibility
