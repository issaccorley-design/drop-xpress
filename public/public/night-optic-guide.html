Choosing the Right Optic for Night Operations

Night vision, thermal, or hybrid — each technology has strengths and hard limits. Pick wrong and you're blind.

1. Night Vision Generations: What Actually Matters

  • Gen 1 — Cheap, noisy, bloom in bright light. Detection ~75–100 m.
  • Gen 2+ — Cleaner image, better low-light. Detection ~200–300 m.
  • Gen 3 (unfilmed) — High FOM (>2,200), minimal halo. Detection 400–600 m.
  • White Phosphor (WP) — Better contrast in mixed lighting. Preferred for urban/night ops.

2. Thermal vs Night Vision: When to Choose Which

Thermal Wins When:

  • Through foliage/smoke/fog
  • Absolute darkness (no ambient light)
  • Detecting heat signatures (people, engines)

Night Vision Wins When:

  • Reading signs/maps
  • Identifying details (antlers, tracks)
  • Low-cost, lightweight setup
